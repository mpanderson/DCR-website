Mission of the BERD Design and Computational Resources Unit

The mission of the BERD Design and Computational Resources Unit is to collaborate and integrate OSCTR clinical research education, mentoring, and career development to enhance training in Epidemiology and Biostatistics related to clinical and translational research. The DCR will provides guided resources such as written and video tutorials along with annotated code to enhance the research quality and productivity of the OSCTR

Data Sets

The DCR maintains a list of sites that house publicly available data sets here. Some of these data sets are governed by data use agreements while others are free to use without such agreements. It is expected that propoer ethical considerations of data use will be followed regardless of a need for data use agreements

Example of Analyses with Code, Frequently Asked Questions, And Software Training

The Office of Advanced Research Computing at UCLA offers a number of excellent resources to help see statistical methods applied in practice. These resources are organized into Annotated Output, Data Analysis Examples, and Textbook Examples. The, annotated output pages are intended to show example of software program output containing footnotes to explain its meaning. The data analysis examples contain illustrations of various statistical analysis methods using a variety of statistical packages (STATA, SAS, SPSS, MPLUS, R). The section for textbook examples shows a variety of analyses referred to in common textbooks worked out for various software packages. They also maintain an excellent Freqently Asked Questions page that provide answers to commonly asked questions about statistical methodology as well as answers to commonly asked questions about using various software packages. Seminars/ Short Courses / Workshops

The OSCTR and BERD host regular Seminars, Short Coursess and Workshops to give researchers opportunities to dive deeper into biostatistical and epidemiological topics and equip them with the resources and tools they need to become familiar with new methodologies. These topics range from basic quantitative methods to more novel methodologies such as missing data analysis. The link above will take you to a list of these seminars, short courses, and workshops which contains links to recorded trainings, workshop notes, computer code, and applicable data sets when available

Software Training

The Office of Advanced Research Computing at UCLA offers a number of excellent software training resoucres for several statistical packages. These resources are organized into R, STATA, and SAS, and SPSS are a few of the common software packages listed. The site provides links to classes and seminars on these software platforms as well as links to learning modules that take users step by step on how to perform various routines in each software package.
